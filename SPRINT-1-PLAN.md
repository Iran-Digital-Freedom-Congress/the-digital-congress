# کنگره‌ی دیجیتال آزادی ایران — Digital Iran Freedom Congress (DIFC)
## Sprint #1: 48-Hour Launch Plan

**Date**: Week of 14–20 April 2026 (exact 48h window TBD)
**Format**: Digital, Slack-based, livestreamed
**Planners**: ~50 core organizers
**Cost**: Near-zero (no physical venue)

---

## 1. The 48-Hour Sprint: What Happens

### Hour 0–2: Opening Plenary (Livestreamed)
- Welcome & framing: what this congress is and isn't
- Current situation briefing (conflict, humanitarian crisis, political landscape)
- Introduce the 8 working groups and their facilitators
- How to participate: Slack channels, Taraaz deliberation, voting
- Code of conduct & conflict resolution mechanism

### Hour 2–24: Working Group Sessions (Parallel Tracks)
Each working group runs in its own Slack channel + scheduled video calls.
Public can observe via livestream; active participants contribute in Slack.

### Hour 24–26: Mid-Point Plenary (Livestreamed)
- Each WG presents interim findings (5 min each)
- Cross-pollination: identify overlaps and gaps
- Open floor for new ideas / course corrections

### Hour 26–44: Working Group Sessions Continue
- Refine proposals, draft outputs
- Conflict resolution facilitators available throughout

### Hour 44–48: Closing Plenary (Livestreamed)
- Each WG presents final outputs (10 min each)
- Taraaz/Polis live deliberation on key questions
- Announce next steps: implementation commitments, follow-up schedule
- Closing statement

## 2. The 8 Working Groups (کارگروه‌ها)

### WG1: نمایندگی دیپلماتیک — Diplomatic Representation
**Output**: Formation of representative groups for Iran's diplomatic issues
- Who speaks for the Iranian people internationally right now?
- What diplomatic positions need to be articulated immediately?
- How to form credible, plural representative groups (not self-appointed leaders)
- Mapping existing diplomatic efforts and gaps
- **Deliverable**: Draft mandate + composition proposal for a diplomatic liaison group

### WG2: ابزارهای مشارکت مردمی — Public Participation Tools
**Output**: Testing and evaluating broad participation tools
- Live test of Taraaz/Polis for consensus mapping during the congress
- Evaluate referendum/polling mechanisms
- Organizing & mobilization tools assessment
- How to reach people *inside Iran* securely
- **Deliverable**: Tool evaluation report + recommendation for ongoing use

### WG3: مشارکت مالی — Financial Participation & DeFi
**Output**: Secure, decentralized financial mechanisms
- Review existing mechanisms (Jomhoor multi-sig, DeXe DAO, crypto donations)
- Sanctions-compliant pathways for diaspora contributions
- How to fund activities inside Iran safely
- Participatory budgeting design
- **Deliverable**: Financial infrastructure proposal + pilot plan

### WG4: شبکه‌سازی امن — Secure Network Building
**Output**: Expand the opposition network safely
- Connecting activists (کنش‌گران), NOT reactionaries (واکنش‌گران)
- Vetting and trust-building mechanisms (multi-sig trust circles from Jomhoor)
- OpSec training and resources
- Using Atlas Iran data to identify potential partners
- **Deliverable**: Network expansion strategy + OpSec guide

### WG5: ایده‌پردازی — Innovation & Ideas
**Output**: Pipeline for attracting, processing, and deploying new ideas
- Open call for proposals during the 48h
- Evaluation criteria for new ideas
- Mechanism for ongoing idea submission after the congress
- Connecting ideas with implementers
- **Deliverable**: Top 10 ideas ranked by feasibility and impact + ongoing submission process

### WG6: فضای مشارکت سیاسی — Political Participation Space
**Output**: Design of a continuous political engagement platform
- What does ongoing political participation look like between congresses?
- Governance structure: who decides what?
- How to keep engagement alive (not just event-driven)
- Integration with existing platforms (Jomhoor, Iranians.Vote)
- **Deliverable**: DIFC continuous engagement charter + quarterly cycle design

### WG7: حل اختلاف — Mediation & Conflict Resolution
**Output**: Professional conflict resolution units for Iran's political space
- Map the key fracture lines in the opposition
- Design mediation protocols for political disputes
- Train and identify mediators
- How to handle organizations/individuals that refuse cooperation
- **Deliverable**: Conflict resolution framework + roster of volunteer mediators

### WG8: دولت دیجیتال / دفاع مدنی / آموزش — Digital Government, Civil Defense & Education
**Output**: Concrete programs in three critical areas
- **Digital Government**: What does a shadow digital governance capability look like?
- **Civil Defense**: How to organize civil protection, mutual aid, and emergency response digitally
- **Digital Education**: Civic education, democratic literacy, counter-propaganda
- **Deliverable**: Program outlines + volunteer sign-up for each track

## 5. The Continuous Cadence: هر هفته، بدون پایان — Every Weekend, Without End

**This is NOT a one-off event.** The first 48-hour sprint is the *kickoff* of a **permanent weekly rhythm**.

### Weekly Structure (Every Weekend)

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│             DIFC Weekly Weekend Session                   │
│             (Saturday + Sunday, every week)               │
│                                                          │
│  SATURDAY                                                │
│  ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐  │
│  │ 10:00–11:00  Opening Plenary (livestreamed)        │  │
│  │   - Week's agenda, news briefing, urgent items     │  │
│  │                                                    │  │
│  │ 11:00–14:00  Working Group Sessions (parallel)     │  │
│  │   - Each WG in its Slack channel + video call      │  │
│  │   - Focus: advance ongoing work, new proposals     │  │
│  │                                                    │  │
│  │ 14:00–15:00  Cross-WG Coordination                 │  │
│  │   - Report-outs, overlaps, resource sharing        │  │
│  └────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘  │
│                                                          │
│  SUNDAY                                                  │
│  ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐  │
│  │ 10:00–13:00  Working Group Sessions (continued)    │  │
│  │   - Drafting outputs, action items                 │  │
│  │                                                    │  │

│  │   - Open floor: anyone can raise issues            │  │
│  │                                                    │  │

│  │   - Assign action items with owners and deadlines  │  │
│  └────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘  │
│                                                          │
│  WEEKDAYS (async, lightweight)                           │
│  ├── Slack: ongoing discussion, async updates            │
│  ├── Action items executed by owners                     │
│  ├── Documentation updated                               │
│  └── Preparation for next weekend                        │
│                                                          │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Why Weekly, Not Quarterly
- **"از کارهای مقطعی خسته‌ای؟"** — Tired of one-off actions?
- Quarterly cycles lose momentum. Weekly rhythm builds **habit** and **trust**
- Each weekend builds on the last — compounding progress
- Participants can join any weekend — low barrier to entry
- If you miss a week, the congress doesn't stop — it keeps going **بدون پایان** (without end)

### Escalation Points (Quarterly Milestones)
Even within the continuous weekly cadence, mark quarterly milestones:
- **Month 1**: Publish first comprehensive output report
- **Month 3**: First "Digital Summit" — extended 48h session, broader outreach, livestreamed keynotes
- **Month 6**: First Coordination Council election
- **Month 12**: Annual review, constitutional convention-style session

### Sustainability
- Weekly facilitation rotates among WG members (no burnout on one person)
- "Core hours" are Saturday + Sunday; everything else is optional/async
- WG facilitators commit to 4-week terms, then hand off or re-commit
- Newcomers get an onboarding session every first Saturday of the month

## 6. What This Congress Is / Is Not (بیانیه)

### IS ✓
- A digital space for structured, productive political engagement
- A 48-hour sprint to produce concrete outputs, not just talk
- Open to all Iranians who oppose the Islamic Republic regime
- Pluralist: all ethnicities, languages, genders, political orientations welcome
- A testing ground for democratic tools and practices
- Transparent: livestreamed, documented, archived

### IS NOT ✗
- Not a governing body or parliament-in-exile
- Not a leadership election or personality showcase
- Not owned by any single organization or political faction
- Not a replacement for IFCongress or other initiatives — complementary
- Not a space for regime apologists or reformists-within-the-system
- Not a debate club — outputs and commitments expected
